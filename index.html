What are we analyzing?

The cryptocurrency market evolves rapidly, with regulations becoming more stringent each year. Routine checks ensure compliance with these evolving legal standards and help avoid potential risks.

Risks

Exploitation of Minors

Entities associated with child exploitation activities.

Illegal Markets

Cryptocurrencies used in unlawful trading platforms and black markets.

Illegal Services

Coins tied to services facilitating prohibited activities such as terrorism or drug trade.

Legal Investigations

Organizations currently under investigation by law enforcement agencies.

Suspicious Exchanges

Trading platforms implicated in scams, unlawful operations, or government asset seizures.

Unauthorized Gambling

Digital currencies linked to illegal gambling websites.

Service Abuse

Cryptocurrencies utilized for offering unapproved or illicit services.

Obfuscation Tools

Coins processed through mixers to disguise their source or destination.

Extortion Funds

Cryptocurrencies obtained via blackmail, extortion, or ransom schemes.

Sanctioned Entities

Organizations facing international or governmental sanctions.

Fraudulent Activity

Digital assets obtained through misleading or deceptive means.

Theft

Cryptocurrencies stolen or obtained without owner consent.

Terrorism Financing

Entities involved in funding terrorist activities or networks.

Suspicious sources

ATM

Cryptocurrencies acquired through cryptocurrency ATMs or similar machines.

Exchange | High Risk

This platform is classified as high-risk if it meets the following conditions:

No KYC: Does not require identification for deposits or withdrawals.

Criminal Ties: The platform has been linked to criminal activity.

Exposure: The platform is often associated with dark web activities or risky services.

Jurisdiction: The platform operates from an area with lax regulations.

Unlicensed: It does not possess a proper operating license.

Liquidity Pools

Smart contracts locking cryptocurrencies to provide liquidity in decentralized exchanges.

P2P Exchange | High Risk

Platforms offering peer-to-peer exchanges without a license, especially those with large transaction volumes and no KYC process, which are vulnerable to money laundering.

Unnamed Service

Refers to unidentified clusters or entities associated with unusual activity but not yet classified.

Trusted sources

Exchange

A platform where users can buy, sell, and exchange cryptocurrencies, operating under relevant licenses for financial services like brokerage or depository. This category includes exchanges handling the bulk of industry transactions, processing around 90% of all crypto funds.

ICO

An Initial Coin Offering (ICO) allows an entity to raise funds by offering new cryptocurrency tokens in exchange for established cryptocurrencies or fiat. While many ICOs are legitimate, there are instances where organizers defraud investors.

Marketplace

Cryptocurrencies used in a legitimate marketplace setting for buying and selling goods or services.

Merchant Services

Services that enable businesses to accept cryptocurrency payments, often involving converting crypto into fiat currency and transferring funds to the merchant's account.

Miner

Cryptocurrencies that have been mined but remain unsold or untransferred.

Other

Cryptocurrencies obtained through means like airdrops, token sales, or other distribution methods.

P2P Exchange

Platforms licensed to allow direct cryptocurrency exchange between users without intermediaries. This excludes platforms based in jurisdictions with weak financial regulations.

Payment Processor

Cryptocurrencies linked to services that facilitate payments for goods and services.

Seized Assets

Cryptocurrencies that have been seized by governmental authorities as part of legal actions.

Wallet

An online wallet used to store and manage cryptocurrencies, which may be custodial, where the service provider holds the private keys for the user. While this can introduce risks, reputable wallets offer better security than individuals managing their own keys.

Sources like darknet marketplaces and other risky entities can be considered high risk.
